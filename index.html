About

I am a fourth-year PhD candidate in Economic Geography at the London School of Economics, funded by the Economic and Social Research Council. My supervisors are Riccardo Crescenzi and Vassilis Monastiriotis.

My research examines how regions can harness Foreign Direct Investment and integration into Global Value Chains to stimulate local economic development, build resilience to shocks and uncertainty, and support the transition to a greener economy. I am particularly interested in the role of regional institutions - especially Investment Promotion Agencies - in shaping whether foreign investments prove transformational for local economies.

My background is in Economics & Geography, having completed a BSc in Economics & Geography from University College London, plus an MSc in Local Economic Development from the London School of Economics. Before starting my PhD, I worked as a policy analyst at Policy in Practice and as a consultant for the World Bank. Alongisde my PhD, I have worked as a Research Officer on the EU Horizon-funded ESSPIN project, leading a Europe-wide survey of Investment Promotion Agencies. I am currently also the Co-PI on an International Growth Centre (IGC) funded research project to measure and understand patterns of green FDI/GVCs into Africa.

Research Interests

Working Papers

Can Regional Institutions Protect FDI During Uncertainty? Evidence from Brexit
Job market paper — Draft available
This paper investigates whether Investment Promotion Agencies' sector-targeting strategies protected inward FDI during the Brexit uncertainty period (2016–2019). Using a difference-in-differences design, I find that pre-2015 IPA targeting increased repeat knowledge-intensive services FDI, with effects concentrated in regions outside South East England and among investments motivated by regional ecosystem factors. These findings suggest that institutional effectiveness during uncertainty operates through ecosystem-building rather than information provision.

Building a New Atlas of Green Foreign Investment Beyond Renewables
With Riccardo Crescenzi and Lee Mager — In preparation
We develop a comprehensive classification of green FDI that extends beyond renewable energy to capture the full breadth of investments contributing to the low-carbon transition.
